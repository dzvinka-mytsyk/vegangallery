Fist of all, PEOPLE

When you work in IT you work with exceptionally smart people.

  • to get the interest in "all things technical" and to keep it going even for decades
  • to endure years of Computer Sience education and continue active self-education for the length of etire career
  • to grasp all those complex and abstract mathemathical and algorithmical concepts
  • natural language processing,
  • and still be able to turn arround and apply them to fix "down to earth" problems

... to do all that and more - you have to be smart.

Second: IT is not about INTERPRETATIONS

In Polytology or Art, Economics or History you might find the "room for opinions or open interpretation based on nothing". But in most cases in IT stuff in truly descreet - things either work or not, compile or not, run or not.

Of cource, there are still places for "opinions", like during the refctoring or choosing the framework, but:

  • people in IT much more often tend to settle those differences using descreet quantified methods. Which I think is again because guys and girls in IT are freaking smart.

Fewer POLITICS

Thanks to lack of interpretation, it always easier to make one's point based on obvious "stuff working or not".
